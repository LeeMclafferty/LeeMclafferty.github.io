Hi, I'm Ron (The Game)

"Hi, I'm Ron", is my first complete game developed in Unreal Engine. This fan game is based on a D&D podcast and showcases my experience with Unreal Engine 5, C++, Blender3D, Niagara particles, and UMG. Through the creation of this game, I gained a deeper understanding of the architecture of a complete game, how the classes in Unreal Engine interact with each other, project scoping, the importance of scalable and reusable code, and overall gameplay programming improvement. I am excited to share my passion for game development and my technical abilities with the world through this first project.

Throughout the project, I aimed to tie together the concepts I had learned and demonstrate the execution flow inside the engine. To understand the lifetime of key classes such as the Game Instance and Game Mode, I implemented features that relied on them. For example, I used the Game Instance to hold the game's overall state, such as the song that played, and the Game Mode for gameplay-specific features such as mission states. I also dipped my toes into shaders and particles by creating all of the water, music, spawning and end game visual effects, including the end game portal.

While game design was not the primary focus of this project, I still aimed to guide the player with particles and animations. For instance, I used an alpha texture and some stretch/squash animations to direct the player in Ron's childhood room.

Overall, this game was a great first project that I was able to successfully scope and complete. Although there are things I would change in retrospect, I am still very proud of the final product and seeing a project through until the end.
